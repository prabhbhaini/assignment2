Biography

Bhagat Singh was a hero of the early 20th-century India independence movement. He was a vocal critic of British rule in India and was involved in two high-profile attacks on British authorities—one on a local police chief and the other on the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi. He was executed for his crimes in 1931 at the age of 23.
